Research identity (fast read)
The through-line in my work is not a set of separate topics, but repeated attempts to answer one bigger question: how do we build tools, interventions, or explanations about minds without being fooled by simplistic claims? I focus on boundary conditions (when effects hold, and for whom), measurement validity/sensitivity (what the method can truly detect), and proportionate interpretation.
psych × ai bridge (non-cosplay version): modelling behaviour responsibly when systems are noisy, individual differences matter, and proxies can create false confidence.

  • The measure shapes the conclusion: validity, confounds, sensitivity, construct/proxy separation
  • Translation under constraints: what can be built/used honestly in messy settings

Signature projects (anchors, not a list)

1) testing when design interventions help vs harm

Method: 2×2×2 factorial (working memory capacity × prompts × layout) on “seductive details”.
Insight: the claim is conditional: learner–design alignment, not universal best practice.

2) measurement validity as the explanation for “contradictory” findings

Method: sleep consolidation + recognition sensitivity work designed around confounds (delay, learning time, list properties).
Insight: literature conflict can reflect weak/insensitive methods; measure ↔ inference must be explicit.

3) the testing effect as boundary conditions + mechanism

Method: processing–retrieval alignment reasoning across material structure + retrieval format.
Insight: educationally useful effects depend on what is being learned and how it must be retrieved.

4) applied intervention evaluation under real constraints

Method: behavioural/CBT‑I style materials analysed with attrition + validity thresholds (minimum diary entries).
Insight: transparency about missingness and adherence is part of evidence quality (no false confidence).

5) conceptual precision (levels-of-explanation)

Method: mereological fallacy analysis (what claims are logically justified in neuro/psych language).
Insight: conceptual hygiene prevents overclaiming; it tightens hypotheses and interpretation.
